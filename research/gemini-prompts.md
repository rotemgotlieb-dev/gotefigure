## R1a — Competitor marketing strategy (artist merch stores)

Research the MARKETING strategies of 15–20 independent artist/illustrator-run apparel and merch brands. Include a mix of: artists selling on Fourthwall, Shopify, and Big Cartel; illustration-led streetwear micro-brands; YouTubers/Instagram artists with merch lines. Prefer brands that started small (solo artist) — include at least 5 that are still small and 5 that grew large, so I can see both stages.

For EACH brand, document with specifics (visit their actual store and socials):
1. Drop model: limited drops vs. evergreen catalog? Drop frequency? Do they use scarcity (timers, limited editions, "gone for now")? Pre-orders?
2. Email/newsletter: do they collect emails, what's the signup hook (discount, early access, art content)? Send frequency and content type if discoverable.
3. Pricing: actual price points per product type (tee, hoodie, hat, print). Do they price premium or commodity? Any anchoring tactics?
4. Storytelling: how do they market the ART and the ARTIST (process content, story behind designs, artist's face/name visible)?
5. Launch/relaunch tactics: anything documented about how they launched or re-launched.

Then synthesize: (a) the 5 marketing tactics that appear most consistently across successful small artist brands, (b) tactics that only work at scale (so I avoid them), (c) a recommended drop-vs-evergreen model and price positioning for a relaunching hand-drawn-art brand. Cite every brand with its store URL. Distinguish what you directly observed on their sites from secondhand claims.

## R1b — Store & site design (how artist stores present art)

Research the STORE/SITE DESIGN of 15–20 independent artist-run apparel/merch stores (same selection criteria as above; you may reuse brands). Visit each store and document:

1. Homepage: what hits first — art, products, or story? Hero treatment?
2. Product grid: how is art-on-apparel presented (flat mockups, on-model photos, lifestyle shots, the artwork itself)? Grid density, filters, collection structure?
3. Product detail pages: image count and types, how the original artwork is shown vs. the product, description voice, size guides, trust elements (shipping/returns clarity)?
4. Brand personality in the UI: hand-drawn elements, animation, custom type, microcopy with voice — or generic theme?
5. About/artist page: how do they present the artist and the process? Does the site connect to their content (YouTube/IG embeds)?
6. Mobile experience: anything notably good or broken?

Synthesize: (a) which design patterns correlate with the stores that feel most premium/credible, (b) the most common design mistakes small artist stores make, (c) the 5 highest-impact design elements a small store should copy, (d) examples of stores that successfully made the SITE ITSELF feel like art (my goal) and what techniques they used. Include store URLs and describe specific pages, not generalities.

## R2a — Growth case studies: tiny art accounts → real merch income

Find 10–15 DOCUMENTED cases of individual artists/illustrators who grew from very small audiences (under ~1,000 followers) into meaningful merch/store sales, between 2022–2026. Strongly prefer: drawing-process creators, daily-art-practice creators ("day N of drawing…"), plein-air/urban sketchers, and artists whose product is their own artwork on apparel/prints.

For EACH case, document:
1. Starting point and timeline: followers when they started posting seriously → when merch income became meaningful. How long did it take?
2. The platform and the inflection point: which platform drove growth, and what specific video/post/series broke through? What format was it?
3. Cadence: how often they posted during the growth phase.
4. Content formula: hook style, video length, voice-over vs. silent, music, how the art process was framed.
5. Monetization sequence: when they introduced products relative to audience size, what they launched first, how they announced it.
6. Source quality: mark each case VERIFIED (interview, podcast, creator's own breakdown, press) vs. INFERRED (you reconstructed it from their public feed) vs. FOLKLORE (claims without evidence).

Synthesize: (a) the common sequence across verified cases (what they did in what order), (b) realistic timeline expectations for daily posting from ~50 followers, (c) the 3 most repeated mistakes in failed attempts if findable, (d) whether daily-practice series ("Drawing Every Day: Day N") specifically have documented success — this is my current format and I need to know if it converts.

## R2b — 2026 platform playbooks for art content (TikTok / Reels / Shorts / YouTube)

Synthesize into: (a) a one-page-per-platform playbook, (b) a recommended primary/secondary platform choice for a solo artist posting daily drawing-process content who wants store traffic, (c) a realistic weekly posting template across platforms for ONE person spending ≤1 hour/day on content.
